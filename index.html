What is the MPAF?
The Mining Performance Assessment Framework is a joint initiative by the University of Edinburgh Business School and AECOM Advisory, commissioned by the Mining 2030 Commission. Its purpose is to enable institutional investors to reduce risk exposure through better, comparable mining performance data, anchored in evidence and calibrated to financial materiality.
Why these design choices?
Every tool that tries to serve everyone ends up serving no one well. These eleven choices are where the position taken changes what the tool is, who can use it, and how credible it becomes. From Mining 2030’s vision to investor-grade risk intelligence
Mining 2030’s ten-year vision and six goals define what responsible mining performance means. From those goals, the MPAF constructs a set of indicators — combining top-down corporate disclosures with bottom-up asset-level data, and calibrated against financial materiality. The result is an outcome-oriented performance signal, anchored in evidence.
Those indicators only become actionable when read within context. A mining company’s performance is shaped by the strategic choices it has made — which minerals to extract, in which geographies, through which ownership structures. The MPAF makes that context explicit through three dimensions: company, mineral, and geography. Comparisons are only meaningful when at least one of those dimensions is held constant.
The ambition is for this framework to anchor the global infrastructure for responsible mining investment — and in time, to sit at the foundation of an International Minerals Agency capable of making that intelligence universally accessible.

The framework’s full power emerges when all three dimensions are engaged simultaneously — by equity stewardship teams, fixed income analysts, and index investors alike. Fixing one or two dimensions — ceteris paribus — is what makes comparisons both rigorous and actionable. These are the questions that require holding context constant before measuring performance.
